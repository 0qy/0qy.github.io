Sprint Two Technical Blog: The Box Model

Margin, border, and padding makes the area around HTML elements, and is very useful in positioning and sizing elements exactly the way that you wanted.

As you can see in the image above, the margin is the amount of the space outside its border. The end of an element's margin would be where it touches its neighbouring elements. One thing that is important to keep in mind is that horizontal margins of two neighbouring elements will add to each other, whereas vertical margins will collapse into eachother. When this happens, the actual calculated margin will simply equal to that of the larger one.

The border is between an element's margin and padding. This is the only one of the three that can be stylised, you can set the color, style and thickness of a border with CSS.

The padding the space between the content and its border, this can be set with px, em, rem or %.
